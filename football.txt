Beneath the sun or floodlight's gleam,
Football lives like a waking dream.
A pitch of green, a whistle’s sound,
Where legends rise and tales are found.
From dusty streets where barefoot kids,
Dream of the goals that Pele did,
To packed arenas roaring loud,
The game unites a global crowd.
The coin is tossed, the captains stand,
The captains’ pennants change their hand.
Kick-off or side? A choice to make,
With every trophy on the stake.
The wingers sprint, so fast and lean,
The grass beneath a blur of green.
They eye the ball with focused stare,
As tension thickens in the air.
The striker runs, a sudden burst,
To quench the goal-scorer’s deep thirst.
A leather thud, a net that shakes—
The stadium roars for all it takes.
A pass through balls, a cross from wide,
A bicycle kick, the keeper’s pride.
A nutmeg here, a sliding tackle,
Breaking through the defensive shackle.
But not for long—the trap is set,
The offside flag, a deep regret.
A sudden break, the counter's on,
Before the chance is even gone.
The keeper dives with frantic grace,
To save the glory of the place.
A fingertip, a post that rings,
The drama that the "beautiful game" brings.
The referee stands, the law in hand,
The bravest person in the land.
A yellow flash, a moment still,
A red card shown—a sudden chill.
The clock ticks on like beating hearts,
As tactics rise and fall apart.
Some play with flair, the "Samba" way,
Some park the bus to save the day.
The gaffer shouts, adjusts the line,
To make the 4-3-3 align.
A high press here, a zonal mark,
Igniting fire from a spark.
A hat-trick scored, the ball is kept,
While defenders sighed and losers wept.
The team applauds, the fans go wild,
For every sprint, for every mile.
And in the stands, the scarves wave high,
Beneath the floodlit evening sky.
The flares, the chants, the painted face,
All speak of football’s grand embrace.
The halftime comes, a moment brief,
To seek out water and relief.
The locker room is filled with heat,
"Don't let them turn, stay on your feet."
Then battle calls, the pace is high,
A thunderbolt from distance flies.
The top-bins hit, the crowd aghast—
A strike of lightning, fierce and fast.
The VAR, the screen, review,
Technology joins football too.
A bated breath, a nervous wait,
Before the ref decides the fate.
The final minutes, lungs on fire,
Fuelled by a desperate, deep desire.
A corner kick, the keeper’s up,
To win the league or lift the cup.
Last minute now, the thrill is peak,
One goal to win, the knees grow weak.
A cross comes in, a header’s met,
The ball is screaming toward the net.
The stadium’s loud like roaring train,
The fans are singing through the rain.
The whistle blows, the game is done,
The battle’s lost, the battle’s won.
The players swap their shirts and smile,
For sport is more than just the file.
From Messi’s touch to CR7’s leap,
To memories that we always keep.
From Maradona’s hand of gold,
To Cruyff’s turns, so brave and bold.
From Zidane’s class to Mbappé’s pace,
The history of the human race.
Whether a World Cup’s heavy weight,
Or Sunday league behind the gate,
The heart remains the same inside,
A game of honor, grit, and pride.
From Anfield’s "Never Walk Alone,"
To the Maracanã, the football throne.
It’s more than just a sport or show,
It’s what we cheer, and feel, and know.
A gift, a flame, a timeless thread,
That lives as long as it is said:
"Pass me the ball, let’s have a go..."
And football's magic starts to flow.
